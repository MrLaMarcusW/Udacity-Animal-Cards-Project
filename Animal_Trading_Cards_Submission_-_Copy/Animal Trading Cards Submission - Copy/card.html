Crocodile

Crocodiles perform the iconic "Death Roll" by grabbing onto their prey with their powerful jaws and then rapidly spinning its body around, often underwater.

  • Scientific Name: Crocodylus Niloticus
  • Averave Length: 14 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 50 years
  • Habitat: Tropical Freshwater

Crocodiles were culturally and religiously important to ancient Egyptians, who considered them to be powerful animals. Crocodiles were associated with the Nile crocodile or the West African crocodile, and were depicted as such or as a human with a crocodile head, such as in the figure of Sobek, the crocodile deity.


Leopard

Leopards ambush their their prey by getting as close as possible, then explosively pouncing on them with a bite to the neck.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera Pardus
  • Averave Length: 7 feet
  • Average Lifespan: 15 years
  • Habitat: Savannah, Rainforest, Mountains

In many African cultures, the leopard is associated with divinity, and is widely regarded as the animal counterpart to the ruler because of its clever hunting skills, its ability to adapt to different environments, and its graceful beauty.
